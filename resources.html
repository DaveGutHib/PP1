Most of gaming history from the early 1980s are all centralised to one country: Japan Mario, Donkey Kong, Pac-Man, Galaxian and Space Invaders were all developed there and exported to the rest of the world.

As a result most of gamings most famous composers are also Japanese: Koji Kondo, Nobuo Uematsu, Yoko Shimomura and Yuzo Koshiro to name but a few

Since Nintendo, the granddaddy of Japanese gamings first home console: The Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), was not widely released nor popular in Europe AND since the Commodore 64 itself wasn't popular in Japan; these two factors combined make it as if there's are alternative unconnected reality that exists in gaming history.

The US and Japan had their 8-bit home gaming consoles, the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) and the Sega Mastersystem These realities would all get consolidated together in the early 1990s when the Super Nintendo and the Sega Megadrive would rise to global domination;
