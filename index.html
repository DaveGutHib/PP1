In August of 1982 the Commodore 64 launched and took the world by storm.

An amazing middle ground between the ludicriously expensive full personal computers of the time, and single-function home gaming consoles like Pong.

The Commodore 64 allowed users to play a variety of games via magnetic casette tape, as well as write code and program their own games in the BASIC language.

For an example lets listen to the original theme song to the arcade version of Commando, released by Capcom in arcades in 1985

Then for comparison here is the Commodore 64 version of Commando by Rob Hubbard

Even though it is considerably less powerful than an arcade machine, the C64's highly regarded sound chip allows for what feels like considerably more complexity and depth to the music.

This music is visualised in an Oscilloscope so you can see each of the three different sound channels at work
